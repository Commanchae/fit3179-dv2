Australian Migration Visualization

Shows detailed information about overseas migration into Australia by state and country


Ever since World War II, increased international migration
into Australia has cultivated the modern Australian culture.

With around one-fifth of Australians being born overseas,
Australia is likely to get even more diverse in the future.

This visualisation serves to show immigration (all Visa types)
in 2022 and past trends.

International Migration Patterns in Australia

The map on the right shows netflow of international
migration into each state in 2022.

The values are shown as number of migrants
per 10,000 people living in the state.

Notable insights are:

   • ACT having high number of migrants despite its size.

   • Victoria having the highest netflow of immigrants
of 230.

   • Tasmania having the lowest netflow of immigrants
of 69.

Top Countries of International Migration into Australia

With most migrants being from Asia, this bar graph
helps pinpoint the specific countries they are from.

The bar graph shows the top countries
migrants are coming from in 2022.

The top country in 2022 is India, with 92,940 migrants.

A slider exists below the chart to show up to 10 countries.

Inflow/Outflow Trends Across Time

This diverging bar chart shows inflow versus outflow over time.

Interestingly, inflow greatly beats outflow throughout most years.

Evidently, the pandemic resulted in a huge temporary shift towards outflows.

However, a major increase in inflow can be seen after
the pandemic subsided.

Net Flow Trends by State

In contrast to the previous plot, this graph breaks
the flow values down into individual states.

This allows you to visualise specific trends in each state.

Similar to the previous graph, these states were greatly
affected by the pandemic, some more than others.

However, after the pandemic subsided, some states' migration numbers skyrocketed, beating their numbers even before the pandemic.
