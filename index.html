Australian Migration Visualization

Shows detailed information about overseas migration into Australia by state and country


Ever since World War II, increased international migration into Australia has cultivated the modern Australian culture.

With around one-fifth of Australians being born overseas, Australia is likely to get even more diverse in the future.

This visualisation serves to show immigration in 2022-2023 and past trends.

International Migration Patterns in Australia

The map on the right shows netflow of international migration into each state.

Notable insights are:

     • ACT having high normalised value despite its size.

     • Victoria having the highest netflow of immigrants at 230.

     • Tasmania having the lowest netflow of immigrants at 69.

Top Countries of International Migration into Australia

With most migrants being from Asia, this bar
helps pinpoint the specific countries they are from.

Specifically, the bar shows the top countries migrants are from as of 2022.

A slider exists below the map to show up to 10 countries.

Inflow/Outflow Trends Across Time

This diverging bar chart shows inflow versus outflow over time.

Interestingly, inflow greatly beats outflow throughout most years.

However, the pandemic resulted in a huge temporary shift towards emigration.

Net Flow Trends by State

In contrast to the previous plot, this graph breaks
the flow values down into individual states.

This allows you to visualise specific trends in each state.

Some states were affected by the pandemic more than others.
